Planet Formation: Where Worlds Begin

Rocky planets, like Earth, are believed to form through two primary mechanisms: core accretion and pebble accretion. By simulating the physics of these processes, we can better understand how the terrestrial planets of our solar system formed and place meaningful constraints on the properties of rocky exoplanets in distant planetary systems.
