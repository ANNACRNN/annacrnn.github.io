Planet Formation: Where Worlds Begin

Rocky planets, like Earth, are thought to form through two main avenues: core accretion and pebble accretion. By simulating the physics involved in each avenue, we can pin down how the terrestrial planets of the solar system formed and place constraints on the characteristics of terrestrial exoplanets in very distant systems.
